Statistics

According to the BBC at least 4.1 billion people use the internet daily which contributes 3.7% of our total carbon footprint for our Earth (1.6 Billon tons).

Many data center companies use fossil fuels in order to keep their own building running due to the increasing amount of internet users and data that needs to be stored daily (BBC).

For Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs) each digital transaction has an average carbon footprint between 33.4kg and 48kg (Earth.org).

Anywhere from 10-30% of a household's carbon footprint comes from the food we eat.

Meats make up approximately 57% of greenhouse gas emissions by food type.

Fruits, grains, and vegetables cotribute to 6.3% of our food emissions combined.

Large appliances like refrigerators emit an annual avergae of 672lbs of carbon dioxide.

Power plants release an average of .889lbs of carbon dioxide per kWh.

Other means of energy production, such as hydroelectric power, release no carbon dioxide, and during production they release less carbon dioxide than power plants.

Based on a research study provided by the United Nations Environment Program, it takes approximately 33.4kg of carbon to produce a pair of jeans from production to retail (WorldBank.org).

"Fast fashion", contributes nearly 1.2 billion tons of carbon dioxide toward our planet each year due to the execsive amount of material discarded (TheGroupAgainstSmogandPollution.org).

Due to the demands of the fashion industry, companies contribute 20% of the waste water that is currently in our oceans. It is also fueled by the decision of placing future factories into countries that lack proper environmental laws (Princeton.edu).

According to the University of Michigan, gasoline releases approximately 19.6lbs of CO2 per gallon.

Averaging 0.74lbs of carbon dioxide per mile driven; driving can greatly contribute to your carbon footprint.

At an average price of $3.26/gal, the average American spends anywhere from $1.4k to $3k on gasoline per year.
